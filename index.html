About Me

After graduating from NYU in May 2013 with a degree in Politics, I became disillusioned by the political process. I was interested in solving public policy problems using logic and data, but I quickly realized that hard work and good ideas are rarely rewarded in the political system. I began looking for a career change before my career even started.

Programming immediately stood out to me. Not only was I attracted to the logic driven nature of coding, I was also excited to learn a skill where hard work and good ideas actually get you somewhere. I began self studying coding and chose to enroll in General Assembly's Web Development Immersive. After a 12 week class studying web development more than 80 hours a week, I'm now employed as an editorial developer at Bloomberg Businessweek. Look at some stuff I might have made
